Meet our founder.

Hey! My name's Drew Segovia. Thanks for coming to our site, I hope you'll soon reach out and schedule a first session with us, and see for yourself what we're all about, but until then, I'll do my best to tell you. I started SoLo in early 2021. Yes, it's a business, but it's also personal for me.

Not too long ago, I was a student preparing to go through the SAT process. It was about midway through my sophomore year, so I had some time, but I started looking into what the next year or so would entail. I went on Reddit, I talked to my friends, I talked to their siblings and what I found out was that everyone and their mother was taking SAT prep classes. I looked into some of those classes, and even the cheapest of those options were running at like 800 or 900 bucks. With prices like that, those options were off the table for me, and that hurt, because I knew I was missing out on something that could really help. ​

About a year later, without thinking back, I apply for a job at a tutoring center near my house. The pay was alright, my boss was fine, and I was happy to be doing good work for students and parents who need help. I worked there for a while and was fortunate enough to work with scores of students and be a part of a lot of positive learning experiences - for me and my students both. But one day while I was paging thought our scheduling program, I came across the user profile for one of my calc students. I clicked on her name, and her most recent transactions with us came onto the screen. That was how I learned that this family was paying $120 every time they dropped their daughter off for a lesson, and it was how I learned that I had actually become a part of the problem that had affected me just a year ago. I honestly didn't know how to react, and I wasn't even quite sure why that was so…offensive to me. It wasn't just the fact that, for a $120 lesson I, the tutor, was making $18. It was something much deeper.

Equal opportunity in education is one of pillars of our society, but prices like that block bright kids from access to the same resources available to the wealthy.

The unfortunate truth is that, if you're paying a company to tutor your son or daughter right now, then you're overpaying. That's why I started SoLo, because with low costs and no over-head, I can provide the exact same instruction you'd get anywhere else at a fraction of the cost.
